Resources

There are many places to find information and ways to get involved in the election.

Get Involved

  • League of Women Voters: Official site. The League is a non-profit organization that engages in a number of activities around voting, from education to registering people to vote and more.
  • Rock the Vote: Official site. Started in 1990 Rock The Vote is a non-profit dedicated to getting young people to vote and participate in the electoral process.
  • Volunteer Match: Official site. This is a site dedicated to helping people find opportunities to volunteer.
    • Opportunities to volunteer related to politics in NYC: See here
  • Become a Poll Worker: See positions

Voter Information and Education

Official and well-known, non-partisan sources to go to get information on the election.

  • New York State Board of Elections: Official site This is an official NYS agency that is tasked with enacting and enforcing all laws related to elections in New York State.
  • New York City Board of Elections: Official site The NYC BOE is the City’s version of the state board and is responsible for running elections in the City.
    • Poll Site Locator: Use tool created by the NYC BOE this gives information on where to vote and what districs you’re in.
  • New York City Campaign Finance Board: Official site. The CFB is an NYC agency that is non-partisan and helps make campaign finance more fair by matching small donor contributions and providing information about campaign finance.
  • Federal Voting Assistance Project: Official site. FVAP is a federal program charged with helping military and US citizens get the tools and information to vote.
  • Vote 411: Official site is created by the League of Women Voters to give out non-partisan information on how to vote.
